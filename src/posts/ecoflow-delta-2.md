## What I Found Using the DELTA 2

I've been testing the EcoFlow DELTA 2 as both a backup power solution and for outdoor use, and it occupies an interesting middle ground in the portable power station market. The 1024Wh lithium iron phosphate battery provides enough capacity for meaningful use cases without crossing into the truly heavy territory of larger units.

The standout feature in my experience has been the charging speed. Getting to 80% charge in under an hour from a wall outlet genuinely changes how I think about using this device. I don't need to plan days ahead or leave it plugged in overnight—I can top it up during a lunch break before heading out. This matters more than the raw capacity in many situations. The unit also accepts solar input, which I've used with portable panels, though charging times vary significantly based on conditions.

For power output, the 1800W continuous capacity covers most of what I've thrown at it. I've run a mini-fridge, charged power tools, and kept laptops and phones going during a brief outage. The 15 ports mean I'm not constantly swapping cables, and the mix of AC outlets, USB-C, and USB-A ports reflects how people actually use devices today. The surge capacity has handled brief high-draw moments when appliances kick on, though I haven't pushed it to the absolute limits.

The LFP battery chemistry is worth noting. These batteries tolerate more charge cycles than standard lithium-ion, which matters for something I expect to use regularly over several years. The longevity claim means I'm not as worried about degradation from frequent use.

Portability is adequate rather than impressive. At 27 pounds, I can move it where needed, and the handles are well-positioned. But this isn't something I'd want to haul on a long hike—it's more suited to car camping or moving between rooms. The footprint is compact enough to store in a closet without dominating the space.

The app control adds convenience for monitoring battery levels and controlling outputs remotely, though I often just use the physical buttons. It's a nice-to-have rather than essential. The screen displays useful information at a glance.

Expandability through additional batteries extends capacity significantly, which provides a growth path if my needs increase. The modular approach makes more sense than buying an entirely new unit.

A few limitations: the fan noise is noticeable under heavy load, the price positions this in the premium segment, and while the capacity works for short-term needs, extended outages would require careful power management or additional batteries.

Overall, the DELTA 2 delivers on its core promise of fast-charging, versatile portable power. It's well-suited for weekend camping, tailgating, or backup power for essential devices during brief outages. The speed and flexibility make it practical for regular use rather than just emergency situations.
