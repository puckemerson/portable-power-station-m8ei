## My Experience with High-Capacity Portable Power

I've been using the EcoFlow Delta Pro as both an emergency backup system and for off-grid applications over several months. This is a serious piece of equipment that sits in a different category from typical camping power stations—it's designed to run household appliances during outages or provide substantial power in remote locations.

## Power Delivery That Handles Real Loads

The standout feature for my use has been the 3600W continuous output with 7200W surge capacity. I've successfully run a window air conditioner, full-size refrigerator, and multiple devices simultaneously without issues. The pure sine wave output means sensitive electronics run without problems. During testing, I powered my home office setup including monitors, computer, router, and LED lighting for a full workday on a single charge. Heavy-duty tools like circular saws and drills operate normally, though I stay mindful of the surge limits.

The base 3.6kWh capacity provides practical runtime for my applications. Running my refrigerator alone, I get roughly 24-30 hours depending on ambient temperature and door openings. For comparison, this is significantly more capacity than entry-level power stations, though users with whole-home backup needs will likely want the expansion batteries.

## Charging Flexibility With Tradeoffs

I appreciate the multiple charging options: AC wall outlet, solar panels, car charging, and even EV charging stations. The AC fast charging genuinely delivers on speed, taking the unit from depleted to 80% in approximately two hours when I need it ready quickly. Solar charging works adequately with proper panel orientation and sunlight, though I've found that partially cloudy conditions significantly impact input rates. The ability to combine charging sources helps speed things up when time matters.

## Practical Considerations

The weight presents real limitations. At close to 100 pounds, I can move it short distances using the wheels and telescoping handle, but loading it into a vehicle requires two people or careful planning. The wheels help on smooth surfaces but struggle on gravel or grass. For permanent or semi-permanent installations, the weight becomes less relevant.

Build quality feels solid with a sturdy case that has handled minor bumps without damage. The screen interface is straightforward for monitoring input/output, battery percentage, and remaining runtime estimates. I cross-reference these with the smartphone app, which provides more detailed information though I've experienced occasional Bluetooth connection delays.

## Where It Fits

This unit makes sense for specific use cases: emergency home backup for critical circuits, RV or van builds, off-grid work sites, or serious outdoor enthusiasts. The price point reflects its capabilities—this isn't an impulse purchase for occasional camping trips. For those needs, smaller units offer better value. But for users who need reliable, high-capacity portable power and can justify the investment, it delivers consistently. I view it as infrastructure rather than a gadget, which shifts the value equation considerably.
